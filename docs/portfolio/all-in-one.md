# All-in-one

An "all-in-one" fund is a single investment vehicle that automatically builds a broadly diversified portfolio of stocks and bonds.
It handles automatic rebalancing and diversification for you, making it ideal for hands-off or beginner investors
All-in-one funds are usually classified as either Target Date or Target Allocation.

- [Total International Stock Market](../index/int-stock/#total-international-stock-market) Index

**Note:**

- Target Date funds are usually available in workplace retirement plans.
- Target Allocation funds are usually **not** available in workplace retirement but useful for in Rollover IRAs, Roth-IRAs, etc.

## Target Date

A Target Date fund automatically shifts your asset mix from aggressive to conservative as you approach the target date by changing the stocks/bonds ratio.

They can be useful leading up to retirement but they tend to become extremely conservative after reaching their target date.

Right before/at retirement, it might be prudent to either:

- Exchange with a newer Target Date fund (e.g. +5 to +15 years)
- Move to a static [Target Allocation](#target-allocation) fund

Here are a listing of Vanguard Target Date funds with their allocation so you can see the drift (stocks/bonds):

- 90/10 at +25 years: (e.g. [VFIFX - 2050](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vfifx))
- 80/20 at +20 years: (e.g. [VTIVX - 2045](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vtivx))
- 75/25 at +15 years: (e.g. [VFORX - 2040](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vforx))
- 70/30 at +10 years: (e.g. [VTTHX - 2035](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vtthx))
- 60/40 at +5 years:  (e.g. [VTHRX - 2030](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vthrx))
- 50/50 at **retired** (e.g. [VTTVX - 2025](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vttvx))
- 35/65 at **-5 years**: (e.g. [VTWNX - 2020](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/mutual-funds/profile/vtwnx))

At **-5 years** the 35/65 (35% stocks / 65% bonds) allocation will have difficulty keeping up with inflation over the long-run.

50/50 is typically considered the most conservative allocation that still protects against long-term inflation.


## Target Allocation

Target Allocation funds maintain a static asset mix such as 60/40 (60% stocks / 40% bonds) regardless of the date.

The fund is periodically rebalanced to ensure that fixed ratio never drifts.
