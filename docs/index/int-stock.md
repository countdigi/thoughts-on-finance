# International Stock

## Total International Stock Market

### Overview

The Total International Stock Market holds the publicly traded companies headquartered outside the United States (~8000 companies).

While investing in the [Total Stock Market](../index/us-stock/#total-stock-market) or the [S&P 500](../index/us-stock/#sp-500) Indexes gives you exposure to corporate America, the Total International Stock Market Index captures the equity landscapes of the rest of the world, spanning dozens of countries across varying stages of economic development.

Funds typically track either the **FTSE Global All Cap ex US Index** or the **MSCI ACWI ex USA Investable Market Index**.

The Total International Stock Market can be decomposed into these two categories:

- 70% [International Developed Markets](#international-developed-markets)
- 30% [International Emerging Markets](#international-emerging-markets)

## International Developed Markets

### Overview

Holds highly established economies with mature financial systems such as Japan, the United Kingdom, Canada, France, Australia, and Switzerland.

Funds typically track the **FTSE Developed All Cap ex US Index** or the **MSCI EAFE Index** (excludes Canada).

### Funds

- [VEA](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/etfs/profile/vea) - Vanguard FTSE Developed Markets ETF

## International Emerging Markets

### Overview

Holds economies experiencing rapid growth and industrialization but carrying higher regulatory, currency, and political risk such as Taiwan, China, India, South Korea, and Brazil.

Funds typically track the **FTSE Emerging Markets All Cap China A Inclusion Index** or the **MSCI Emerging Markets Investable Market Index**.

### Funds

- [VWO](https://investor.vanguard.com/investment-products/etfs/profile/vwo) - Vanguard FTSE Emerging Markets ETF
