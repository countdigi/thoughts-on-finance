# US Bond

## Total Bond Market

### Overview

The Total Bond Market represents the broad, investment-grade, taxable fixed-income market in the United States.

It holds ~ 70% US government debt and 30% investment-grade corporate bonds.

It is considered an intermediate bond index with a current average duration of 6 years.

Funds typically track the **Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Bond Index**.

### Interest Rates vs Fund Value

Using the [US 10 year Treasury](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/US10Y) as a benchmark, a change an interest rate inversely affects the fund's value, e.g.:

- +1% interest rate = (-1 * 6y avg duration * +1% yield) = -6% value
- -1% interest rate = (-1 * 6y avg duration * -1% yield) = +6% value

Note that as bonds mature, new bonds added to the index will enjoy the higher interest rate making up for the loss in value over time (~ average duration).
